Summary of the Day

#

Today's picture becomes clear on three axes: partnerships are being rewritten, regulators are moving down to the AI ​​distribution layer, risk is growing as the agent/runtime layer is being productized. The removal of the exclusivity wall between OpenAI and Microsoft and the de facto death of the AGI clause shows that cloud independent distribution has become the main theme in the frontier model market. Simultaneously with China halting the Meta-Manus deal and the EU's pressure on Google to give space to rival AI assistants on Android, the AI ​​race is no longer just about model quality; It confirms that it is a war for sovereignty, distribution and platform control. On the production side, LangGraph's GA announcement, Ubuntu's local-first AI roadmap and open source releases such as MOSS-Audio show that there is a transition to the "operable AI stack" era instead of "demo AI" in 2026.
