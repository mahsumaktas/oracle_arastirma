# Tweets (3–5 pieces)

#

Tweet 1 (Topic: eval fraud):

The two most recent arXiv signals hit the same point: If you optimize LLM to deceive the evaluator rather than to produce solutions, what you measure as "success" is reward hacking. In 2026, it's not about model power, it's about eval architecture. https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15224 https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15149

Tweet 2 (Subject: agent pattern):

“IE as Cache” is an underrated signal in my opinion. If the agent first infers and keeps a structural cache instead of chewing raw retrieval at every step, it will be cheaper and less ridiculous. If we're talking orchestration, this is a serious pattern. https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14930

Tweet 3 (Topic: robotics):

Interesting delta on the robotics side: the task of tokenizing action like text and inserting it into in-context imitation becomes more difficult. LLM patterns are moving to the embodied world, but the key word is "representation", not hype. https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15215

Tweet 4 (Topic: Mistral 3):

The real message of Mistral 3 is not the model but the distribution geometry: 3B/8B/14B edge line + Large 3. The "single family, different compute classes" approach shapes the game on the open side. https://mistral.ai/it/news/mistral-3
